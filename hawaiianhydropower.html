Hawaiian Hydropower

About

Hawaiian Hydropower is grounded in research done at loko iʻa, fishponds developed over a thousand years ago by native Hawaiians. Loko iʻa are masterpieces of indigenous innovation, capable of producing upwards of 2 million pounds of fish per year. However, only 28 of the original 488 loko iʻa exist today. Influenced by a confluence of humanism and colonialism, loko iʻa were deemed primitive and suppressed for years; I was inspired to revitalize this ancient practice. Hawaiian Hydropower researches and implements non-invasive hydropower in loko iʻa to supply renewable energy for nearby communities.
